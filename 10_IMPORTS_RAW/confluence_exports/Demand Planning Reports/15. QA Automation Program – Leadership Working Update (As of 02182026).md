## 7. Key Risk: Unplanned Support Load & Decision Blockers

### What’s Changing

- **Legacy → IDP migration:** Plans migrating without QA notification are causing reactive rework and regression noise; migration ownership and advance notification need confirmation.
- **Prime v3 charter:** Scope expanding organically without an explicitly agreed charter (full successor to V2 vs. UE/IDP-only).
- **Pipeline direction:** API and Performance automation are technically ready but blocked on GitLab vs. GitHub decision—nightly execution cannot begin until finalized.
- **Capacity & intake:** Increasing test volume and ad-hoc support requests are putting pressure on capacity and JIRA hygiene.

### Risk

Sustained unplanned support and delayed decisions can slow roadmap delivery, delay pipeline expansion, and reduce predictability. Part-time BA/scrum admin (30–40%) for intake, JIRA hygiene, and backlog health has been requested as team grows.

### Leadership Alignment Needed

Alignment on **migration ownership**, **Prime v3 charter**, **pipeline direction**, **capacity protection** (BA/admin approval), and **intake discipline** (“no ad-hoc QA work outside JIRA”) is essential to sustain momentum and protect delivery focus.
